What is Universal Credit?

Universal Credit is a type of welfare benefit provided by the UK government to help individuals and families with low income or no income at all. It's a single benefit that replaces six existing benefits, including income-based Jobseeker's Allowance, income-related Employment and Support Allowance, and Housing Benefit. Universal Credit is designed to support people who are working but struggling to make ends meet.

Am I Eligible for Universal Credit as a Self-Employed Individual?

To be eligible for Universal Credit, you must meet certain criteria. As a self-employed individual, you'll need to demonstrate that you're actively working and earning an income. You'll also need to show that you're unable to support yourself through your earnings alone.

Here are some key factors to consider:

* Income: You'll need to earn below the Universal Credit threshold, which varies depending on your circumstances. For the 2022-2023 tax year, the threshold is £1,084 per month for single claimants and £1,560 per month for joint claimants. * Business expenses: As a self-employed individual, you may be able to claim business expenses as deductions from your income. This can help reduce your earnings and increase your eligibility for Universal Credit. * Tax: You'll need to be registered for Self Assessment with HMRC and have filed your tax return for the current or previous tax year.
