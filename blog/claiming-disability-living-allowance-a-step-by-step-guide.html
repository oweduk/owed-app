Claiming Disability Living Allowance: A Step-by-Step Guide

Introduction to Disability Living Allowance

Disability Living Allowance (DLA) is a UK government benefit designed to help individuals with disabilities or health conditions with the extra costs they may face. If you or someone you care for has a disability, you may be entitled to claim DLA. In this article, we will guide you through the process of claiming Disability Living Allowance, providing you with the information you need to navigate the system successfully.

Eligibility Criteria for DLA

To be eligible for DLA, you must meet certain criteria. These include: - Being under 16 (for children) or 65 or under (for adults) - Needing help with personal care or having difficulty walking - Being in the UK, Ireland, or another European Economic Area (EEA) country, or having been in one of these areas when you claimed - Having a condition that has lasted for at least three months and is expected to last for at least six months

How to Claim Disability Living Allowance

Claiming DLA involves several steps: 1. Gather Necessary Information: Before you begin, ensure you have all the necessary details, including your National Insurance number, bank account details, and information about your disability or health condition. 2. Download and Complete the Claim Form: You can download the DLA claim form from the UK Government website or request one by phone. Fill out the form carefully, answering all questions as fully as possible. 3. Attach Supporting Evidence: Include any medical evidence or reports from your doctor or other healthcare professionals that support your claim. 4. Submit Your Claim: Send your completed form and supporting documents to the address provided on the form.

What to Expect After Claiming

After submitting your claim, you will receive a decision from the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP). This could take several weeks. If your claim is successful, you will be awarded one of two components: - Care Component: This is paid at one of three rates: lowest, middle, or highest, depending on the level of care you need. - Mobility Component: This is also paid at one of two rates: lower or higher, depending on your mobility needs.

Appealing a Decision

If your claim is rejected or you are awarded a lower rate than you expected, you can appeal the decision. To do this: - Request a Mandatory Reconsideration: Ask the DWP to review their decision. - Appeal to a Tribunal: If the reconsideration does not change the decision, you can appeal to an independent tribunal.

Real-Life Example: Claiming DLA for a Child

Emma's son, Jack, has cerebral palsy and requires constant care. Emma claimed DLA on his behalf, providing detailed information about his condition and the care he needs. After a successful claim, Jack was awarded the highest rate of the care component and the higher rate of the mobility component, significantly helping with the costs of his care.

Conclusion and Next Steps

Claiming Disability Living Allowance can seem daunting, but with the right guidance, you can navigate the process with confidence. Remember, you are not alone; many people are entitled to DLA but do not claim it. If you believe you or someone you care for may be eligible, do not hesitate to start the claim process.
