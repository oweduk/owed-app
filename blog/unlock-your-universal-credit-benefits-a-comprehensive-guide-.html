Understanding the Basics of Universal Credit ------------------------------------------

Universal Credit is a financial benefit designed to support individuals who are out of work or working part-time and have limited income. To qualify, you'll need to meet certain conditions, including:

* Being under state pension age * Being available to start work * Being actively seeking employment * Having a limited income or no income at all * Being living in the UK

Step 1: Check Your Income -------------------------

Your income is a critical factor in determining your eligibility for Universal Credit. To qualify, you'll need to have a limited income or no income at all. This includes:

* Earnings from employment * Self-employment income * Pensions * Benefits, such as housing benefit or council tax benefit * Any other income that's not exempt

If you're unsure about your income, use our benefit calculator tool to get an estimate of how much you might be eligible for.

Step 2: Check Your Expenses ---------------------------

In addition to your income, your expenses will also be taken into account when determining your eligibility for Universal Credit. This includes:

* Rent or mortgage payments * Council tax * Utility bills * Food and other living costs

You'll need to provide detailed information about your expenses to ensure an accurate assessment.

Step 3: Check Your Availability for Work -----------------------------------------

To qualify for Universal Credit, you'll need to be available to start work. This means:

* Being actively seeking employment * Being willing to take up employment * Being available to start work at short notice

You'll need to provide evidence of your job search and availability to start work.

Step 4: Check Your National Insurance Contributions ------------------------------------------------

Your National Insurance Contributions (NICs) will also be taken into account when determining your eligibility for Universal Credit. You'll need to have made sufficient NICs to qualify for the benefit.
