Who is Eligible for Universal Credit?

To be eligible for Universal Credit, you must meet certain criteria:

* You must be 18 years old or over * You must be under State Pension age * You must be working or looking for work * You must be on a low income or out of work * You must have £16,000 or less in savings (excluding your main home and some other exceptions)

What Documents Do I Need to Claim Universal Credit?

To claim Universal Credit, you'll need to provide some essential documents, including:

* Your National Insurance number * Proof of identity (such as a passport or driving licence) * Proof of address (such as a utility bill or tenancy agreement) * Proof of income (such as a payslip or P60) * Proof of savings (such as a bank statement)

How to Claim Universal Credit: A Step-by-Step Guide

Claiming Universal Credit is a relatively straightforward process that can be completed online or over the phone. Here's a step-by-step guide to help you get started:

1. Check if you're eligible: Before you start the application process, check if you meet the eligibility criteria. You can use the UK Government's online tool to check your eligibility. 2. Gather your documents: Make sure you have all the required documents ready, including your National Insurance number, proof of identity, and proof of income. 3. Apply online: You can apply for Universal Credit online through the UK Government's website. You'll need to create an account and provide your details, including your name, address, and National Insurance number. 4. Provide evidence: You'll need to provide evidence of your income, savings, and other relevant information. You can upload scanned copies of your documents or provide them over the phone. 5. Attend an interview: You may be required to attend an interview with a work coach to discuss your job search and any barriers you're facing. This is a great opportunity to get support and guidance on finding work. 6. Wait for a decision: Once you've submitted your application, it may take 5-7 working days for a decision to be made. You'll receive a letter or email with the outcome of your application.
