What is a State Pension?

A state pension is a government-funded benefit designed to provide financial security for individuals who have worked and paid National Insurance contributions throughout their lives. In the UK, the state pension is typically paid to individuals who have reached State Pension Age (SPA), which varies depending on your date of birth.

Am I Eligible for a State Pension?

To be eligible for a state pension, you typically need to have:

* Paid National Insurance contributions for at least 10 years * Reached State Pension Age (SPA) * Not already received a state pension from another country

However, there are some exceptions and additional requirements, such as pension credits and deferred pension. To determine your eligibility, you can use the UK Government's state pension calculator or contact the Pension Service directly.
