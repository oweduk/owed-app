What is Personal Independence Payment (PIP)?

Personal Independence Payment (PIP) is a benefit for people of working age who need help with daily life due to a disability or long-term health condition. It's designed to help you with the extra costs of living with a disability.

What is Disability Living Allowance (DLA)?

Disability Living Allowance (DLA) is a benefit for children and young people under 16 who need help with daily life due to a disability. DLA is being replaced by PIP for people over 16, but it's still available for those under 16.

Eligibility Criteria for PIP and DLA

To be eligible for PIP or DLA, you'll need to meet certain criteria. Here are some of the key things you'll need to consider:

* Your condition: You'll need to have a condition that affects your daily life, such as a physical disability, mental health condition, or chronic illness. * Your mobility: You'll need to be able to demonstrate that you have difficulty with mobility, such as walking, standing, or using a wheelchair. * Your daily living: You'll need to be able to demonstrate that you have difficulty with daily activities, such as bathing, dressing, or using the toilet. * Your age: You'll need to be under 66 to apply for PIP, or under 16 to apply for DLA.

How to Apply for PIP and DLA

Applying for PIP or DLA can seem daunting, but it's easier than you think. Here are the steps you'll need to follow:

1. Check your eligibility: Use the government's online tool to check if you're eligible for PIP or DLA. 2. Gather evidence: You'll need to gather evidence to support your claim, such as medical records, letters from your doctor, or witness statements. 3. Fill out the application form: You'll need to fill out the application form, which will ask you about your condition, mobility, and daily living. 4. Submit your application: Once you've filled out the form, you'll need to submit it to the DWP. 5. Wait for a decision: The DWP will review your application and make a decision. This can take several weeks or months.
